About Us

Our mission to glorify God by raising people and rebuilding communities

Nashery’s Global Initiative (NGI) is a registered non-governmental, non-political, and interfaith humanitarian organization committed to transforming lives and communities across Nigeria and beyond. Founded on February 13, 2024, by Dr. Teme-ebi Iseoluwa Nashery, NGI was birthed out of a passion to raise a new generation of compassionate, purpose-driven changemakers.

We believe that everyone; regardless of tribe, religion, or background, has something powerful to contribute to the healing and progress of our world. NGI brings together passionate volunteers from diverse walks of life to serve humanity through outreach, education, health campaigns, skill empowerment, youth leadership, and community development.

Our mission is to empower individuals and communities by nurturing values, building capacity, and mobilizing resources that promote human dignity, peace, and sustainable impact. We operate through well-structured state chapters and zonal teams across Nigeria, with active youth-led volunteerism at our core.

Through love, service, and unity, we are building a national movement where no one is too small to make a difference, and no community is too forgotten to be transformed.

Together, we are Transforming Lives Through Compassion, Faith and purpose.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our organization

What is NGI?

Nashery's Global Initiative (NGI) is a non-governmental, non-political, and Interfaith humanitarian movement committed to transforming lives across communities in Africa and beyond. We are passionate about social changes, community development, and youth empowerment. NGI is driven by compassion, integrity, and the commitment to bring meaningful change through people-focused initiative.

When was NGI founded, who is the founder, and why was it established?

Nashery’s Global Initiative (NGI) was officially founded on February 13th, 2024 by Dr. Teme-ebi Iseoluwa Nashery. It was born out of a deep passion to see young people rise into purpose-driven leadership, impact their communities positively, and live for something greater than themselves. The founder, after years of mentoring youths and observing the increasing social, moral, and leadership decline in many communities, felt a divine burden to establish a movement that will:

1. Unite passionate changemakers across faiths and regions
2. Promote compassion, service, and social transformation
3. Empower volunteers to take bold steps toward healing and rebuilding society.

NGI started with a small team and has now grown into a national movement driven by selfless leaders and volunteers across Nigeria.

How can I become a volunteer?

You can apply to become a volunteer by completing our online application form. After submission, our volunteer coordinator will contact you for an orientation session. We require all volunteers to complete basic training before participating in field activities.

Is NGI a religious organization?

No. NGI is interfaith and non-political. We welcome both Muslims and Christians and emphasize peace, love, and service.

Do you partner with other organizations?

Yes, we actively seek partnerships with churches, faith-based organizations, and community groups. Our current partners include the Collen Mashawana Foundation and several local churches across Nigeria.

How can I stay updated on NGI's activities?

Subscribe to our newsletter, follow us on social media, or join our Telegram group for regular updates. All our communication channels are listed in the contact section.
